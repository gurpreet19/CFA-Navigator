Welcome to Your CFA Journey

You are about to embark on one of the most rigorous and respected paths in finance. As a beginner located in India targeting 2027, you have a strategic advantage: Time. This dashboard breaks down the complex CFA Institute requirements into actionable steps.

Three Levels

Level I (Knowledge), Level II (Analysis), Level III (Synthesis). You must pass them sequentially.

300 Hours

Your Roadmap to 2027

Since you are starting in Feb 2026, targeting a 2027 completion is aggressive but possible. A more realistic, high-probability path finishes in early 2028. Below is the Standard Track designed for working professionals or full-time students.

Step 1: Immediate Actions (Feb - Mar 2026)

  • Admin Ensure your Passport is valid. This is non-negotiable.
  • Strategy Create an account on cfainstitute.org (free). Check for "Access Scholarship" windows (usually open in Jan/Feb or Sept - apply immediately if open).
  • Study Start learning Quantitative Methods using free YouTube resources (Mark Meldrum 2017 videos are old but concept-solid) to build a base before paying fees.

Step 2: Level I Preparation (Apr - Oct 2026)

Target Exam: Nov 2026

Register for the Nov 2026 exam by May/June to get the "Early Bird" fee. This gives you a solid 6 months of study.

  • Phase 1 (Apr-Jul): Read curriculum, watch videos. Focus on Understanding.
  • Phase 2 (Aug-Sep): Practice questions (Question Bank).
  • Phase 3 (Oct): Mock Exams. Aim for 70%+ scores.

Step 3: Level II (Feb - Aug 2027)

Target Exam: Aug 2027

If you pass L1 in Nov 2026 (results out in Jan 2027), you register immediately for L2 Aug 2027.
Warning: Level II is mathematically harder. Valuation is the core.

Step 4: Level III (Sep 2027 - Feb 2028)

Target Exam: Feb 2028

This is the final boss. Focuses on Portfolio Management and Wealth Planning. Half the exam is essay-based (structured response).

Included in your fee. It contains the official text, practice questions, and mock exams. Don't ignore this!
