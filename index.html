CFA for Absolute Beginners

If you are new to the CFA (Chartered Financial Analyst) program, here is your foundational checklist for the Indian market.

The Basics

Passport is Critical

You need a valid international travel passport to register and sit for the exam in India. Aadhaar or PAN card are not accepted for the exam day.

Approved Calculators

Only two models: TI BA II Plus (recommended) or HP 12C. Start using one immediately to build muscle memory.

Education Rule

Must be in the final year of your degree (or completed). Level II registration requires you to have graduated.

Level I Preparation Strategy

"The biggest mistake is starting with the hard topics. Build confidence first."

1 Master Quant & Financial Statement Analysis first.
2 Aim for 300-350 hours of study over 6 months.
3 Take 5-7 Mock Exams. Speed is key (1.5 mins per question).

The CFA Curriculum (All 3 Levels)

The program covers 10 main functional areas. Click the levels to see what's inside.

Level I: The Building Blocks

Ethics & Professional Standards (15-20%)

Code of Ethics, Standard of Professional Conduct. Most important section!

Financial Statement Analysis (13-17%)

Balance Sheets, Income Statements, Inventory, Taxes, and Leases.

Quantitative Methods (6-9%)

Time Value of Money, Probability, and Statistics.

Asset Classes (Equity/Fixed Inc)

How to value stocks and bonds at a basic level.

Level II: The Valuation Expert

Moving from conceptual knowledge to applying formulas. Level II is often considered the hardest "math" level.

Equity Valuation (10-15%)

FCFF, FCFE, Dividend Discount Models. Picking stocks professionally.

Corporate Issuers (5-10%)

Capital structure, M&A, and Corporate Governance.

Derivatives (5-10%)

Forwards, Futures, Options, and Swaps.

FSA (Level II)

Intercorporate investments, Pensions, and Multinational Ops.

Level III: The Portfolio Manager

The "Real World" application. Focus is on managing wealth for individuals and institutions.

Portfolio Management (35-40%)

IPS creation, Behavioral Finance, Asset Allocation.

Fixed Income (15-20%)

Advanced Yield Curve strategies and Credit analysis.

The Essay Component

Morning session is structured response (written). You must explain logic, not just pick options.
